I am currently a third-year PhD student in the [STIM](https://www.stim.minesparis.psl.eu/en/home/) laboratory at Mines Paris - PSL and École Normale Supérieure, under the supervision of Bruno Figliuzzi and Peter Neri. I am broadly interested in inverse problems, with my current work focusing more specifically on simplex-constrained inverse problems, especially in settings where observations can be modeled as mixtures or convex combinations of underlying components.

My research initially focused on hyperspectral unmixing and has progressively broadened toward generative modeling, structured latent representations, and model explainability.

Before starting my PhD, I completed an [M.Sc. in Automatic Control and Robotics](https://fc.sorbonne-universite.fr/nos-offres/master-automatique-robotique-parcours-ingenierie-des-systemes-intellignets-isi/) in the Intelligent Systems Engineering (ISI) track at Sorbonne Université, after earning a [B.Sc. in Mathematics](https://sciences.sorbonne-universite.fr/formation-sciences/offre-de-formation/licences/les-l2-l3-nos-huit-disciplines-de-licence/licence-0) from the same university.
